Why is IT important?

Information technology helps to build and grow the commerce and business sector and generate the maximum possible output. The time taken by different sectors to generate business is now minimized with advancements in Information technology. It provides electronic security, storage, and efficient communication.

To conduct the work, Information technology needs computer applications. Computers connect IT to the different organizations of the world. It helps the employees to maintain records of their numerous clients of various companies. It helps patients to contact physicians online and take advice regarding their health problems. Also, records of patients can be managed properly by the system.

To collect information, programming/coding, data conversion, data communications retrieval and storage, and system analysis are used. Even the education sector has been dramatically changed with the arrival of Information Technology. To run a business in the right way and generate expected outcomes, computers, software, and the internet helps a lot.

Companies are now having virtual vaults which is a new form of storage system which allows users to keep or withdraw their documents. The IT department is providing a strong communication system to communicate effectively.

The use of computers and the internet increases the quality of education. The pedagogical method of teaching and learning has been improving and IT contributes to improving school systems, student activities, and teaching practices.

Students are more open to learning with modern technologies and focusing on online teaching more. Their learning methods are depending on live interaction with the teachers and special classes for special children.

The aura of Information technology can be seen in almost all fields including work, learning, leisure, and health. From ministries to classrooms, every sector uses IT for the best results.

Parent Section

  • Technology has significantly transformed the way parents raise and support their children. Here is a brief overview of how technology helps parents:


    Parenting Apps: Numerous parenting apps help parents organize schedules, track milestones, monitor health and nutrition, and manage household tasks, streamlining parenting responsibilities and keeping things well-organized.


    Remote Monitoring and Safety: Parents can use smart devices and monitoring tools to ensure children's safety and well-being. Baby monitors, GPS trackers, and parental control apps provide peace of mind while allowing kids some independence.


    Educational Support: Technology offers educational resources like interactive learning apps, online tutoring, and educational websites. Parents can supplement their children's education and provide extra learning opportunities at home.


  • It is essential to strike a healthy balance and be mindful of screen time and its potential effects on children's development and well-being. Responsible and thoughtful use of technology can undoubtedly enhance parenting experiences.

    Access to a vast amount of parenting information and resources online.

    Parenting apps for organizing schedules, tracking milestones, and managing tasks.

    Convenient communication with children through video calls, messaging, and social media.

    Remote monitoring tools, such as baby monitors and GPS trackers, for ensuring child safety.

    Educational resources, including interactive learning apps and online tutoring, to support children's education.

    Online parenting communities and support groups for connecting with other parents and seeking advice.

    Remote work opportunities for greater flexibility in balancing work and family life.

  • It is crucial to be mindful of its potential drawbacks and find a balance between its use and meaningful human interactions.

    Information overload

    Digital distractions

    Sedentary lifestyle

    Privacy and security concerns

    Negative content exposure

    Social comparison and pressure

    Sleep disturbances
